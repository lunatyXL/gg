The Global Challenge

We are facing a dual crisis: water scarcity in agriculture and rampant plastic pollution clogging our ecosystems.

Water Scarcity

Traditional irrigation wastes up to 60% of water. In arid regions, this defines the line between harvest and hunger.

Plastic Waste

Millions of plastic bottles end up in landfills daily. They take 450 years to decompose, poisoning our soil and oceans.

High Costs

Commercial drip irrigation systems are expensive to install and maintain, making them inaccessible to small-scale farmers.

The DIY Revolution

The "Bottle Drip" method repurposes waste to create life. By perforating used plastic bottles and burying them near plant roots, we create a slow-release watering system.

  • 90% Efficiency: Water is delivered directly to the root zone.
  • Zero Cost: Utilizes trash that is readily available.
  • Evaporation Control: Sub-surface delivery prevents sun evaporation.
  • Scalable: Works for a single balcony plant or an acre of crops.

Solving Social Problems

This isn't just about gardening; it's about social equity, food security, and community resilience.

Food Security

Enabling families in "food deserts" to grow nutrient-rich vegetables with minimal resources, ensuring no child goes to bed hungry.

Economic Empowerment

Smallholder farmers reduce overhead costs on water and expensive equipment, increasing their profit margins and financial stability.

Education & Awareness

At St Xavier's Burdwan, we use this method to teach biology, physics, and responsibility, turning students into eco-conscious citizens.

How to Build Your Own

Simple steps to transform waste into a resource.

1

Collect & Clean

Gather used PET bottles (1L or 2L). Remove labels and wash thoroughly to prevent mold.

2

Perforate

Use a hot needle to poke 3-4 small holes near the bottom and sides of the bottle.

3

Bury

Dig a hole next to your plant. Bury the bottle up to the neck, leaving the cap accessible.

4

Fill & Cap

Fill with water. Close the cap loosely to control flow speed. Refill every few days.
